# Scheduling Rules — Soft Constraints and Special Cases

These are strong preferences and structural rules that should be followed as closely as possible. Unlike hard constraints, violations here represent a degraded (but not invalid) schedule, except where noted.

---

## 1. Golden Weekends

- Every EM resident should receive **1 golden weekend (Saturday + Sunday both off) per 2-week block**, or **2 golden weekends per 4-week block**.
- The two golden weekends in a 4-week block do **not** need to be alternating. For example, a resident may have off the first and last weekends of the block while working the two middle weekends.
- **Priority**: EM residents have higher priority for golden weekends than Off-Service Rotators (OSRs).
- **Exception**: If a resident requests so many days off that fulfilling those requests conflicts with granting a golden weekend, the golden weekend requirement may be forfeited. This is the only case where an EM resident should not receive their allotted golden weekends.

---

## 2. Day-Off Requests

- Residents may request **2 days off per 2-week block**, or **4 days off per 4-week block**.
- Requests should be honored as much as possible.
- When a resident requests more than their allotted days off, they should provide a priority order. If no priority is listed, confer with the resident directly.
- Off-service rotators are entitled to the same number of request days as EM residents.
- Scheduling should **prioritize residents who have made specific requests** over those who have not made requests when distributing desirable days off.

---

## 3. Night Shift Load

- There are no official regulatory limits on consecutive night shifts.
- **Soft guideline**: Schedule residents for no more than **6 consecutive night shifts** for wellness/quality of life.
- This issue most commonly affects PGY2 residents, who carry the heaviest night shift burden.
- Night shift distribution goal: **30% interns / 70% PGY2s** for Bellevue nights.

---

## 4. Wednesday Special Staffing

Wednesdays have a unique staffing model due to a protected academic conference in the morning. No residents may be scheduled for morning shifts on Wednesdays.

### Bellevue — Wednesday Afternoon Shifts:
| Shift | Type | Hours |
|-------|------|-------|
| D1 | PGY3 senior | 1p–8p |
| D3 | PGY3 senior | 1p–8p |
| D4 | PGY3 senior | 1p–8p |
| d2b | Junior (must be PGY2) | 1p–10p |
| d4 | Junior swing | 2p–12a |
| d5 | Junior swing | 2p–12a |
| PT4 | Peds PECC | 4p–12a |
| PB4 | Peds PES | 4p–12a |

- The `d2b` shift on Wednesday **must be staffed by a PGY2** (not a PGY1 or OSR).
- Peds shifts PT4 and PB4 run the same hours as other days (4p–12a).

### Tisch — Wednesday:
- **1 Wildcard (WC) shift only**: 1p–9p, not team-specific.
- Operations will flex this resident to whichever team needs support.
- No morning shifts at Tisch on Wednesdays.

### Brooklyn — Wednesday:
- **No shifts at Brooklyn on Wednesdays.**

### Day Float (DF) — Wednesday:
- DF shift at Bellevue shifts to **12p–8p** on Wednesdays (instead of the usual 8a–4p).

---

## 5. Float Shifts

- Float shifts are **optional** — they do not need to be scheduled.
- They may be assigned to senior residents (PGY3/PGY4) when all required shifts have been filled and residents still have remaining shift capacity.
- Float shifts may be scheduled on **any weekday** only.

### Float Shift Details:
| Shift | Site | Hours (PGY4) | Hours (PGY3) |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------|
| DF | Bellevue Day Float | 8a–4p (12p–8p on Wed) | 8a–4p (10 hrs) |
| TF | Tisch Float | 8a–4p | 8a–4p |
| KF | Kimmel Float | 12p–10p (PGY3) / 12p–8p (PGY4) | 12p–10p |

---

## 6. Adjunct Shifts

Residents on certain off-service rotations (AnUS, Psych, Hand, Tox) are permitted to work "adjunct" shifts in the ED on weekends only. These shifts help fill coverage gaps.

### Rules:
- Adjunct shifts may ONLY be worked on **weekends** (Saturday or Sunday).
- A resident may work at most **1 adjunct shift per weekend**.
  - Allowed: Saturday of weekend 1, Saturday of weekend 2 (different weekends).
  - NOT allowed: Saturday + Sunday of the same weekend.
- Adjunct shifts should be **avoided when possible**, used only when necessary for coverage.
- **Tox residents**: Maximum **2 weekends** in a 4-week block (more intensive rotation).

---

## 7. Teaching Shifts

- Teaching shifts are relevant during **July, August, September, and October** when medical students rotate through.
- The scheduler does not need to create the medical students' schedules.
- However, senior residents must be assigned to **teaching shifts (DT at Bellevue, TT at Tisch, both 8a–4p)** that align with the medical students' schedules.
- Medical student schedules are provided by UME faculty.

---

## 8. Nights — Tuesday Soft Protection

- If possible, residents should have Tuesday nights off.
- One way to help achieve this: schedule an off-service resident on Team 5 swing on Tuesdays (freeing up an EM resident from that slot).

---

## 9. PGY4 Morning Report

- On **Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday**, the PGY4 assigned to the `D2a` shift at Bellevue is expected to give a morning report (designated in the original schedule with pink lettering).
- This is a structural role, not just a shift — the D2a PGY4 on these days should be someone capable of running morning report.

---

## 10. OSR Golden Weekend Deprioritization

- Off-service rotators have lower priority for golden weekends compared to EM residents.
- In the ideal case, OSRs should also receive golden weekends, but this is secondary to EM resident preferences.

---

## 11. Extra Shifts / Owed Shifts

- If all required shifts are covered and a resident has not yet worked their maximum shift count (9 per 2-week block), the extra shifts can be banked as **"owed shifts"**.
- Alternatively, additional **float shifts** (DF/KF) can be created for seniors to work.

---

## Priority Ordering for Scheduling Decisions

When trade-offs are necessary, use this priority ordering:

1. **Hard constraints** (duty hours, eligibility) — never violate
2. **Required shift coverage** — all mandatory shifts must be staffed
3. **EM resident day-off requests** (within allotted allowance)
4. **EM resident golden weekends**
5. **OSR day-off requests**
6. **OSR golden weekends**
7. **Soft wellness preferences** (night shift limits, Tuesday nights)
