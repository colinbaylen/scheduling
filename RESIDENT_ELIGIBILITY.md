# Resident Eligibility — Who Can Work Which Shifts

This document defines which residents are eligible for which shifts, based on PGY level, site, anchor status, and role. Use this as the primary reference for constraint validation when assigning shifts.

---

## Resident Classifications

### By Training Level
| Class | PGY Year(s) | Also Called |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Intern | PGY1 | Junior |
| — | PGY2 | Junior |
| — | PGY3 | Senior |
| Chief | PGY4 | Senior |

- "Junior" refers to PGY1 **and** PGY2 collectively (36 residents total: 18 PGY1 + 18 PGY2).
- "Senior" refers to PGY3 **and** PGY4 collectively.
- Shift name casing indicates level: **lowercase** = junior shift, **UPPERCASE** = senior shift.

### Off-Service Rotators (OSRs)
- OSRs are residents from other specialties temporarily rotating through the Bellevue ED.
- All OSRs are treated as **PGY1 equivalents** for scheduling purposes.
- OSRs are classified as either **anchor** or **non-anchor** based on their home specialty:

| Anchor Services | Non-Anchor Services |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Ortho | Psych |
| Internal Medicine | OMFS (Oral/Maxillofacial Surgery) |
| Plastic Surgery | Ophthalmology |
| EM (own residents) | Prelims |

- **Anchor OSRs** can fill any junior anchor shift at Bellevue.
- **Non-anchor OSRs** can ONLY fill shifts explicitly designated as NON-ANCHOR (Team 2 non-anchor shifts only).

### PGY1 Site Assignment Detail
- Each PGY1 is assigned to **one specific site** (either Bellevue or Tisch) for the duration of their block.
- They should work **primarily** at their assigned site.
- A small number of shifts at the other site is acceptable if operationally necessary, but should be minimized.

### Key Bellevue PGY2 Requirements (Hard Rules)
1. There must **always** be a PGY2 on Team 2 **day shift** (the "examining resident") — on both weekdays AND weekends.
2. On every Bellevue **night shift**, there must be a PGY2 on **Team 1 (n1)** AND a PGY2 on **Team 3 (n3)**.
3. The `d2a` weekday shift must always be a PGY2.
4. The `d2b` shift on **Wednesdays** must be a PGY2.

## Senior (PGY3 / PGY4) Shift Eligibility

### General Rule
- Senior shift names are **UPPERCASE**.
- PGY3 and PGY4 can generally fill the same senior shifts, but with **different shift durations** and **some PGY4-exclusive shifts**.

### PGY4-Exclusive Shifts (Bellevue)
| Shift | Role | Times |
|-------|------|-------|
| D2a | Team 2 senior day | 8a–4p |
| D2a (pink) | Team 2 + morning report | 8a–4p (M/T/Th/F) |
| D2p | Team 2 senior swing | 4p–12a |
| N2 | Team 2 senior night | 11:45p–8a |

> These three shifts at Bellevue Team 2 are **PGY4 only**. PGY3s may not fill them.
